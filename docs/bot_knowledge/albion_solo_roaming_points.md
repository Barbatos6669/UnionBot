# Albion Solo Roaming For Points

Keywords: solo roaming solo black zone solo bz solo mists might favor points conqueror challenge battleaxe mercenary jacket open world mobs camps coffers corrupted dungeons roads green chests tracking gathering solo build

Use this when members ask how to solo roam, farm points, build a reliable solo
set, or help the guild when nobody is online to group.

Goal:

- A solo player should build a repeatable loop, not chase every fight.
- The loop is: cheap reliable set, lethal Mists or black-zone route, farm safe
  objectives, gather while moving, bank often, repeat.
- Exact Might/Favor/season-point values change with patches. Do not promise
  exact numbers. Tell members to check the in-game Conqueror's Challenge,
  current map objective tooltip, and caller notes.

Recommended starter build: Battleaxe sustain farmer

- Weapon: Battleaxe.
- Off-hand: Torch for cheap attack speed and lower cost. Muisak is more damage
  but costs more and can create mana pressure.
- Helmet: Guardian Helmet for emergency defense and safer remounts. Fiend Cowl
  is a more aggressive swap when hunting players because purge can remove boots
  or defensive buffs.
- Armor: Mercenary Jacket for Bloodlust sustain.
- Shoes: Soldier Boots for safer escape, or Scholar Sandals if energy is the
  main problem.
- Cape: normal cape while learning. Thetford or Martlock are upgrades after the
  route is profitable.
- Potion: Invisibility Potion for escape or Healing Potion for budget learning.
- Food: Roast Pork for sustain. Beef Stew is a damage swap when the player is
  comfortable.
- Start cheap: 4.1, 5.0, or 5.1. Upgrade only after the player comes home
  profitable several runs in a row.

Why Battleaxe:

- Good sustain for mobs and camps.
- Affordable non-artifact weapon.
- Useful in PvE and still has kill pressure in PvP.
- Forgiving for new solo roamers because the set can recover health while
  clearing.

Good alternatives:

- One-handed Spear + Torch: balanced kiting, mobility, and safer spacing.
- Druidic Staff + Torch: strong PvE camp farmer, but avoid most PvP unless the
  player knows Nature Staff well.
- Dual Swords: budget mobility and brawling, but less forgiving if the player
  wastes cooldowns.
- Bloodletter: strong escape and execute for experienced players, but weaker
  as a pure camp farmer unless the player already knows the weapon.

Best solo point/farm targets:

- Lethal Mists: camps, weakened wisps, open-world mobs, resources, coffers,
  Knightfall Abbey only if the player accepts the no-mount risk, and spiders
  only if uncontested.
- Black-zone open world: upgraded mobs, coffers, treasure chests, enchanted
  resources, and opportunistic low-risk kills.
- Roads of Avalon: green chests and resources can be good, but watch for small
  groups. Solo Roads are higher risk because exits shift and groups roam them.
- Corrupted Dungeons: good 1v1 practice and loot/fame. Better for players who
  want controlled solo fights, less ideal for pure low-stress roaming.
- Tracking: small targets can be done solo or with 1-3 players if the member
  has the tracking tool unlocked.
- Solo dungeons: safer filler when the map is crowded, but usually weaker than
  good open-world mobs/camps for active roaming.

Solo route loop:

1. Leave with one affordable set, empty bags, food, potions, and one gathering
   tool if the player can use it.
2. Enter lethal Mists or a black-zone route from portal/HO.
3. Clear one camp, objective, or upgraded mob pocket.
4. Gather enchanted nodes on the path, but do not sit still too long.
5. Skip bad fights. Dismounted players waiting near exits or objective edges
   usually mean trouble.
6. Bank after a good loot spike, full food timer, or any time the player feels
   tempted to say "one more objective."
7. Rebuy the same set after deaths. Do not change five pieces every run.

Fight rules:

- Do not fight with mobs hitting you unless you planned it.
- Do not fight with boots down.
- Do not chase into fog, choke, or objective edges.
- If the enemy has higher IP and better cooldowns, leave.
- Fight gatherers, low-health players, overextended players, or players who
  already used boots/defensives.
- Survival is progress. A banked 150k run beats a 600k death.

UnionBot short answer:

"For solo points, run a repeatable lethal Mists/BZ loop. Start Battleaxe,
