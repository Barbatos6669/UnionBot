# Albion Zone And Transport Decision Tree

Keywords: zone zones blue yellow red black lethal full loot non lethal knocked down transport hauling route risk safe city red zone black zone roads caerleon black market scout gank faction flagged

Use this when members ask "is this safe?", "can I lose my gear?", "should I
transport this?", or "why is this route risky?"

Zone risk:

- Blue zones are lowest risk. Normal PvP is not the focus; faction flagging can
  still create faction fights. PvE knockdowns cause durability loss, not full
  loot.
- Yellow zones are practice-risk zones. Normal PvP knockdowns do not full-loot
  the player, but faction flagging and special content can change the fight
  context. Treat yellow-zone faction as combat practice, not safe AFK travel.
- Red zones are lethal when the player dies to hostile/faction PvP. Equipment
  and inventory can drop. Red zones show hostile flagged player count, but that
  count is not a guarantee of safety.
- Black zones are full-loot PvP with open hostile rules. Assume every unknown
  player can be a threat. Roads of Avalon and Outlands logistics should be
  treated like lethal route planning.

Transport decision tree:

1. Is the route only between safe royal cities, not touching Caerleon, red zone,
   black zone, Roads, or hideout logistics? Lower risk.
2. Does it touch Caerleon, Black Market, red zone, black zone, Roads, or HO
   routes? High risk. Use scouts, split loads, and do not over-invest in one run.
3. Is the route based on the bot market/arbitrage post? Remember the post is a
   buy-order/sell-order suggestion, not a promise instant market prices still
   exist.
4. Is the load more than the player can afford to lose? Do not run it solo.
5. Is the player faction flagged? Warn them faction status can create extra PvP
   exposure.

Officer-style answer:

- "That route is risky because it touches lethal or high-traffic zones. Use a
  scout, keep your load replaceable, and do not treat the suggested price as an
  instant-buy guarantee."
- "If you are not sure whether you can lose the set, check the zone color and
  event post before you leave."

Sources summarized: StudioLoot zone guide; Union market policy.
