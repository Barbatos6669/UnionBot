# Albion Group Combat And Calling

Keywords: albion group combat caller shotcaller calling engage disengage reset bomb clap catch pierce purge defensive rotation healer tank support dps caller comms vod review focus fire resilience clump

Group PvP is about creating a clean window, not everyone pressing buttons as
soon as they see a target.

Simple fight structure:

1. Scout and choose the fight.
2. Catch or force enemy movement.
3. Purge or force defensives.
4. Pierce/debuff the kill target or clump.
5. Buff damage if the comp has it.
6. Dump burst together.
7. Execute or call reset.

Common calls:

- "Hold damage" means save major damage skills.
- "Defensives now" means survive the enemy engage, not chase.
- "Pierce now" means debuff before damage lands.
- "Purge now" means remove key enemy buffs or defensives.
- "Dump" means commit damage into the called window.
- "Reset" means stop chasing, regroup, heal, and prepare the next rotation.

Member discipline:

- Do not talk over the caller during engages.
- Call important information briefly: enemy clump, healer caught, purge used,
  cleric popped, tank in backline, healer needs peel.
- Do not make long speeches mid-fight.
- If you die, say what killed you once, then let the fight continue.

Comp basics:

- Tank/catch starts or controls the fight.
- Pierce/debuff makes the target easier to kill.
- DPS saves burst for the called window.
- Healer keeps the group alive through enemy pressure.
- Support handles purges, defensives, utility, or peel.

After content:

- VOD review should focus on fixable habits: positioning, cooldown use, engage
  timing, target choice, comms discipline, and whether the comp matched the
  content.

Bot caution:

Do not overrule the caller. If an event has a posted comp or caller instruction,
that event post is source of truth.
