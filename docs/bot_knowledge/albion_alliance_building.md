# Albion Alliance Building

Keywords: albion alliance alliances guild diplomacy cta timer timers prime time season points point sharing disarray zerg debuff hideout territory castle outpost nap spy spies guest trial recruitment small guild inactive guild

Alliance building in Albion is not about collecting as many guild tags as
possible. A strong alliance is a small-to-medium group of guilds that share
timers, geography, Discord habits, and willingness to show up.

Core alliance pillars:

- Timer alignment. Guilds need overlapping active hours for CTAs, hideout
  defenses, territory fights, castles, outposts, faction warfare, and planned
  black-zone content.
- Geographic focus. Do not stretch the alliance across random portals and
  continents. Pick a practical home area and build around the timers you can
  actually defend.
- Shared organization. Use one Discord planning system for event signups,
  shotcaller planning, voice channels, regear, bounties, route reports, and
  officer coordination.
- Clear contribution rules. Every guild should know what it is expected to
  provide: bodies, shotcallers, scouts, economy, transports, crafters, regears,
  route scouting, hideout defense, or objective attendance.
- Active roster standards. Guilds should purge inactive members and keep their
  own leadership honest. A large dead roster is worse than a smaller active
  roster.
- Trust and security. Vet new guilds, limit private channel access, and do not
  reveal logistics or objective plans to guests until they have earned trust.

Small guild policy:

- Small guilds are not automatically bad.
- A small guild is worth keeping if it brings reliable shotcallers, a timezone
  the alliance lacks, scouts, economy support, transport help, faction callers,
  or a tight group that actually attends content.
- A tiny inactive guild with no caller, no Discord adoption, no objective
  attendance, and no economy contribution is usually dead weight.
- Do not inherit another alliance's inactive guild pile. Recruit the active
  guilds that want structure, not the whole graveyard.

Season-point and large-alliance caution:

- Albion has used alliance point-sharing systems where season points earned by
  a guild are shared between that guild and the alliance. Exact current splits
  can change, so verify current rules in-game, on official patch notes, or with
  officers before making season policy.
- The strategic lesson is stable: too many low-contribution guilds can dilute
  value and create resentment if one or two guilds do all the work.
- Albion also has zerg/Disarray-style mechanics intended to reduce the power of
  very large allied groups in the same fight. Exact thresholds and values can
  change, so avoid giving precise current numbers unless verified.
- A focused alliance plus diplomacy/NAPs can be healthier than a bloated
  alliance where nobody knows who is responsible for anything.

New guild vetting checklist:

- What UTC timers can they mass?
- How many real active members can they bring, not just roster count?
- Who are their shotcallers and officers?
- What content do they already run weekly?
- Are they willing to use the Union Discord, registration, LFG, and voice
  planning systems?
- Can they follow comp, IP, regear, and objective rules?
- Are they willing to help with castles, outposts, faction, black zone, roads,
  transports, scouting, hideout defense, or economy work?
- Are they looking to contribute, or are they looking for free protection?

Recommended onboarding flow:

1. Invite interested guild leaders and members as guests.
2. Run content together before any official alliance invite.
3. Watch attendance, comms, attitude, and ability to follow plans.
4. Give limited channel access until trust is proven.
5. Make expectations explicit before inviting the guild.
6. Keep a probation period for new guilds.

Good short response to interested guilds:

"Thanks for reaching out. You are welcome to join our Discord as guests and run
some content with us while we feel things out. We are building an organized
open-world PvP alliance, so before anything is official we want to see timer
fit, activity, comms, and whether both sides work well together."

Union stance:

Example Alliance should prefer active, organized, teachable guilds over
inflated numbers. The alliance should be built around reliable content, shared
planning, and objective participation, not paper membership.
