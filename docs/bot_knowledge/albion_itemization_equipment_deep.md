# Albion Itemization And Equipment Deep Reference

Keywords: albion itemization equipment item power ip tier enchantment quality durability repair overcharge siphoned energy weapon armor offhand cape bag mount potion food awakening awakened weapons attunement trait ip cap softcap

Use this file when members ask what gear means, what IP means, why a build is
not just a weapon, why overcharge matters, why repair costs hurt, or what an IP
cap/softcap does.

Equipment structure:

- Weapons define most active combat identity. One-handed weapons can use
  off-hands; two-handed weapons trade off-hand stats for stronger weapon design.
- Head, chest, and shoes provide abilities and passives. Chest armor strongly
  shapes offense/defense identity.
- Cape, bag, food, potion, mount, and swaps matter. A "build" is the whole kit,
  not just weapon plus armor.
- Tools and gathering gear are economic loadouts, not normal fighting sets.

Item Power:

- Item Power is affected by tier, enchantment, quality, mastery, specialization,
  and other item-specific systems.
- IP increases the strength of many gear stats and ability values, but it does
  not replace skill, comp fit, or caller timing.
- A lower-tier high-spec set can be better value than an expensive low-spec set.

Tier and enchantment:

- Albion gear uses tiers. Enchantment raises item power and often changes price
  dramatically.
- Enchantment notation like 6.1 means tier 6, enchantment 1.
- Each enchantment level usually adds a meaningful IP jump. Verify exact current
  values in game when numbers matter.

Quality, durability, repair:

- Quality improves item stats and value.
- Durability loss increases repair costs. Knockdowns and deaths can damage gear
  depending on content rules.
- Repair before content. Do not join lethal content with half-broken gear unless
  the caller knows and accepts it.

Overcharge:

- Overcharge temporarily increases item power at a cost and with item-risk
  consequences.
- It is usually a caller decision for serious fights, not a random habit for
  cheap content.
- If an LFG says overcharge, members should bring the correct energy and be
  ready to use it on command.

IP caps and softcaps:

- Some content limits effective IP. IP above the cap may be reduced rather than
  fully counted.
- Non-lethal and instanced PvP modes often use caps or softcaps to keep fights
  controlled.
- Do not judge a build only by sheet IP if the content softcaps gear.

Awakened weapons:

- Awakened weapon systems add traits and progression to qualifying weapons.
- T6+ awakened weapons have special attunement rules and full-loot area
  relevance.
- Expensive awakened weapons should not be brought to risky content unless the
  owner understands the loss risk and the caller approves.

Consumables and mounts:

- Food is a planned buff. Omelettes, stews, sandwiches, pies, salads, and other
  food families serve different roles.
- Potions are tactical buttons. Resistance, gigantify, poison, healing, cleanse,
  invisibility, and energy choices change how a player survives or kills.
- Mounts decide travel safety, carry weight, dismount risk, and group speed.
  A wrong mount can ruin transport or delay form-up.

UnionBot answer style:

- If someone asks "is this build good?", ask what content it is for.
- If someone asks "what IP do I need?", point to the LFG minimum and explain
  that softcaps can change effective power.
- If someone asks "why did I lose expensive gear?", explain lethal risk,
  durability, repair, and bring-what-you-can-afford rules.
