# Albion Master Reference

Keywords: albion master reference overview wiki official beginner advanced destiny board fame ip spec zones pvp pve economy crafting gathering guild warfare faction mists roads hellgate corrupted dungeon hideout territory castle season

Use this file as the broad map. For exact item stats, current patch numbers,
spell values, market prices, fame values, or meta builds, answer with caution
and tell the member to verify in game, in the current LFG comp, or with a caller.

Core identity:

- Albion is a classless sandbox MMO. A player's weapon, armor, cape, consumables,
  mount, and swaps define the role they can perform.
- Most gear and market value comes from player gathering, refining, crafting,
  hauling, selling, losing gear, and replacing gear.
- Fame is progression experience. Combat, gathering, crafting, faction, PvE,
  PvP, and other activities all feed different progression paths.
- Item Power is gear strength. Higher IP helps, but role fit, defensive usage,
  timing, positioning, and composition often matter more.

Risk map:

- Safe/blue content is for learning, economy setup, practice, and new-player
  direction.
- Yellow content is low-risk practice. Knockdown rules are safer than lethal
  death, but durability and time can still be lost.
- Red and black content should be treated as lethal unless the specific instance
  says otherwise. Bring gear you can afford to lose.
- Roads, lethal Mists, black zones, red-zone flagged combat, many Hellgates, many
  Corrupted Dungeons, and most open-world PvP can become full-loot situations.

Progression map:

- Destiny Board tracks unlocks and specialization.
- Learning Points can accelerate progress after a fame threshold on a node.
- Fame Credits help move excess combat fame into other combat lines.
- Specialization increases power with specific item lines. A high-spec cheap set
  can outperform a low-spec expensive set in real content.

Content map:

- Solo: solo dungeons, Mists, corrupted dungeons, gathering, transporting,
  market work, tracking, faction roaming, and some Abyssal Depths.
- Duo/trio: duo Mists, Hellgates, Roads chests, ganking, small objectives, and
  Abyssal Depths.
- 3-5: static dungeons, group dungeons, ganking, roads, faction outposts, arena,
  crystal arena, small-scale PvP.
- 6-10: roads farming, gank groups, faction warfare, outpost pressure, small
  black-zone roams, castles/outposts harassment, and comp practice.
- 10-20+: Avalonian dungeons, larger roads groups, castles, objectives, faction
  masses, ZvZ, hideout/territory operations.

Combat map:

- Kill windows matter more than random damage. Catch, force defensives, purge or
  wait, pierce/debuff, buff damage, burst, execute, reset.
- Focus Fire/Resilience reduces dogpile damage when many players hit one target.
- AoE Escalation rewards hitting multiple enemy players with AoE damage.
- Crowd Control has resistance/diminishing behavior. Do not stack random CC
  before the real damage call.
- Disarray and cluster queue mechanics affect large fights. Big groups need
  discipline, role assignments, and clean shotcalling.

Economy map:

- Gathering produces raw resources. Refining turns raw resources into crafting
  materials. Crafting produces gear and consumables.
- Resource return rate, local production bonuses, focus, journals, laborers,
  city bonuses, hideout bonuses, taxes, and hauling risk decide profit.
- Market/arbitrage advice should be treated like stock-trading suggestions:
  place buy orders at target buy prices, haul only if risk is acceptable, and
  list sell orders at target sell prices. Do not promise instant prices.

Guild operations map:

- Guilds and alliances need aligned timers, voice discipline, shared Discord
  operations, roster hygiene, logistics, and activity.
- Hideouts, territories, castles, faction objectives, roads routes, and regear
  systems require planning. Last-minute pings are weaker than claimed timers and
  prepared comps.
- If a member asks what to do, route them toward the event board, LFG, content
  role pings, or an officer depending on the question.
