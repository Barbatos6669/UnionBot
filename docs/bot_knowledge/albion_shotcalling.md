# Albion Shotcalling Guide

Keywords: shotcalling shotcaller caller calling zvz small scale engage reset comms target calls defensive caller offensive caller vod review terrain objective fight plan

Use this when members ask how to shotcall, how to become a caller, what a
shotcaller does, or how to practice leading content.

Core idea:

- A shotcaller turns information into simple orders the group can follow.
- The caller does not need to be the best mechanical player, but they must be
  clear, decisive, and willing to review mistakes.
- Calling is learned by doing. Start with low-risk content, then scale up.

Shotcaller responsibilities:

- Set the objective before the group leaves.
- Pick a comp that fits the content and group skill.
- Explain comm rules before the fight.
- Track terrain, enemy position, cooldown windows, and retreat paths.
- Call when to engage, hold, kite, reset, chase, or leave.
- Review deaths/fights afterward without turning it into blame.

Before content checklist:

- What is the objective? Farm, gank, outpost, core, vortex, castle, territory,
  static dive, faction, Roads, or training?
- Who is main caller?
- Who is backup caller if main caller dies?
- Who calls defensives or peel?
- Who scouts?
- What comp are we running?
- What is the minimum IP and required swaps?
- Where do we bank or reset?
- What fights do we avoid?

Simple call format:

- Direction: "north", "east choke", "back west", "hold bridge".
- Target or area: "their healer", "front clump", "southwest clothies".
- Timing: "in 3, 2, 1".
- Action: "catch", "purge", "pierce", "dump", "defensives", "reset".
- Result: "finish", "loot fast", "do not chase", "remount".

Example:

"Hold damage. Tanks look east choke. Catching in 3, 2, 1. Caught front clump.
Purge now. Pierce now. Dump damage. Finish healer. Reset west. Do not chase."

Fight phases:

- Posture: group is positioning and looking for a good angle.
- Poke: light damage to force movement or cooldowns.
- Engage: synchronized catch and damage window.
- Defensive response: enemy or friendly group uses defensives to survive.
- Counter-engage: the other side spends offensive cooldowns after surviving.
- Brawl: cooldowns are mixed and both groups are trying to create pressure.
- Reset: group backs up, heals, waits cooldowns, and looks for next window.

Caller habits:

- Use short words.
- Repeat critical calls.
- Avoid explaining during the fight.
- Do not panic-call every red name.
- Know the terrain before committing.
- Call exits before the group needs them.
- If the engage fails, call the reset quickly.
- Own bad calls in review and move on.

Training path:

1. Lead easy fame farms or faction groups to practice movement and voice
   control.
2. Lead cheap small-scale roams where deaths are acceptable.
3. Record fights or ask someone to stream for VOD review.
4. Review one question after each fight: what was the missed call?
5. Add complexity slowly: backup caller, defensive caller, scout calls, comp
   swaps, objective timers.

Union guidance:

- New callers should be encouraged, not roasted.
- Backseat calling is only useful if the main caller asked for it or the group
  is about to wipe from missing information.
- Members should follow the caller during content, then give feedback after.
- A clean bad call is better than five people making five separate plans.

UnionBot short answer:

"Shotcalling is turning chaos into short, followable orders. Start by leading
low-risk content, set the objective, name a backup caller, keep comms clear, use
simple calls like hold/catch/purge/dump/reset, and review one mistake after each
fight. The caller's job is not to be perfect; it is to make the group move
together."
