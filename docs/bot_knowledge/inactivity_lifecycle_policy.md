# Inactivity And Lifecycle Roles

Keywords: inactive inactivity lifecycle role roles alumni recruit member veteran guest vc voice stat movement activity purge nudge loa leave of absence recover status

UnionBot uses lifecycle roles to keep the server readable and to help officers
separate active members from people who are drifting.

Common lifecycle roles:

- Recruit: newer home-guild member.
- Member: standard active home-guild member.
- Veteran: established active home-guild member.
- Inactive: home-guild member who appears inactive by configured activity
  checks.
- Alumni: former home-guild member.
- Guest: non-home-guild visitor, faction guest, diplomacy contact, or manually
  reviewed non-member.
- Alliance: member of the home alliance or approved allied guild.

## What Inactive Means

Inactive is not meant to shame a member.

It usually means the bot did not see enough activity signals, such as:

- Not joining voice for the configured window.
- No meaningful Albion stat movement for the configured window.
- Not participating in tracked guild content.

The exact thresholds are server configuration and can change. Do not promise an
exact number unless the live bot status/config says it.

In this server's current policy direction, VC activity is important because the
guild runs organized content through Discord voice. Stat movement matters too,
but voice shows whether someone is connected to guild content.

## Visibility And Recovery

Inactive members may have visibility closer to Guest-level access so public
channels stay cleaner and active operations stay easier to manage.

Recovery should be simple:

- Register/sync if needed.
- Join guild voice and guild content again.
- Sign up for events and attend.
- Ask an officer if the role looks wrong.
- Use LOA if they know they will be away.

If a member asks why they became Inactive, answer calmly:

"The bot marked you inactive because it did not see enough tracked activity.
Join voice/content again or ask an officer to review it if that looks wrong."

## Officer Tone

Avoid purge-threat energy. Data shows activity improves better through clear
timers, steady content, and easy paths back than through panic pressure.

Use the bot to identify who needs a nudge, not to start public arguments.
