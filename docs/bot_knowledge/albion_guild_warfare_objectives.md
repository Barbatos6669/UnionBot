# Albion Guild Warfare And Objectives

Keywords: albion guild warfare objective objectives guild alliance hideout ho headquarters hideout hq power core territory castle castle outpost outpost season points conquerors challenge faction warfare bandit assault fortress smart cluster queue disarray zvz outlands roads diplomacy roster

Use this file when members ask about guild objectives, alliance structure,
hideouts, territories, castles, faction objectives, season points, or why the
guild needs organized LFG/timer claims.

Guild and alliance basics:

- Guilds organize players, taxes, territories, hideouts, roles, regears, and
  content.
- Alliances coordinate multiple guilds, but only useful alliances create actual
  activity, shared timers, voice discipline, and trust.
- Small inactive guilds can add social reach, but they also add management,
  security, and season-point dilution problems if they do not contribute.

Hideouts:

- Hideouts are guild bases in Outlands and Avalonian Rests.
- They support staging, storage, repair/logistics, and local operations.
- Hideouts need defense, fueling/maintenance knowledge, access control, and
  planned timers.
- Headquarters Hideouts have special protection/power behavior. Exact attack
  and protection rules are patch-sensitive, so verify before planning a war.

Territories:

- Territories are claimable open-world spaces.
- Attacking or defending territory requires timers, form-up, composition, scouts,
  shotcalling, and enough active members.
- A guild should not talk about holding territory if it cannot form reliable
  groups on the relevant timers.

Castles and castle outposts:

- Castles and outposts are open-world guild objectives.
- They are not just PvE camps. Capturing or holding them can pull enemy groups.
- Scouts, comp, caller, escape route, and post-fight banking matter.

Season points and Conquerors' Challenge:

- Season systems reward guild/player activity through objectives and challenges.
- Season point rules change across updates. Do not give exact current point
  math unless verified from current season information.
- A good guild uses season objectives as activity goals, not as empty scoreboard
  chasing.

Faction Warfare:

- Faction Warfare is objective-based PvP on the Royal Continent.
- Players enlist for city factions and fight over outposts, regions, faction
  objectives, and campaign pressure.
- Bandit Assault style events pull large groups and can be chaotic. Use faction
  channels and roles when organizing faction content.
- For home-guild/alliance operations, faction warfare can bring outside faction players into
  Discord, but access and role separation still matter.

Large fight mechanics:

- ZvZ needs assigned roles and a caller. A random mass with no comp is not a
  serious alliance operation.
- Disarray affects oversized Outlands groups.
- Smart Cluster Queue can prevent everyone from entering a crowded zone and can
  favor better-organized groups.
- Bigger is not automatically better if it becomes slow, disorganized, or
  vulnerable to spies.

Operational requirements:

- Align timers. If guilds cannot play the same timers, they cannot defend the
  same objectives reliably.
- Plan comps. Members need to know weapon, armor, swaps, IP floor, food,
  potion, mount, and form-up time.
- Use Discord. Voice and signups are not optional for serious lethal content.
- Purge inactive rosters. A large roster that does not form is weaker than a
  smaller roster that actually shows.
- Separate permissions. Guild, alliance, guest, faction, and diplomacy access
  should not all see the same sensitive channels.

UnionBot answer style:

- If asked why timer claims matter, say they stop last-minute chaos: caller,
  comp, IP, roster, voice, and timer are decided before content starts.
- If asked whether to invite a guild to alliance, mention activity, timer
  overlap, PvP willingness, roster hygiene, Discord use, security, and trust.
- If asked about an objective, route toward LFG/event planning and officer
  confirmation rather than improvising a war plan in chat.
