# Albion IP, Spec, And Progression

Keywords: albion ip item power spec specialization mastery fame combat fame gear tier enchantment quality overcharge repair reaver destiny board build weapon armor cape food potion

IP means Item Power. It is a quick measure of how strong equipped gear is, but
it is not the same as skill, comp, or good timing.

What affects IP:

- Gear tier and enchantment. Example: 6.1 means tier 6 with enchantment level 1.
- Item quality, such as normal, good, outstanding, excellent, or masterpiece.
- Mastery and specialization on the Destiny Board.
- Some temporary effects such as overcharge when used.
- The specific item line. Weapon IP matters for damage/healing output; armor IP
  mostly affects survivability and the strength of that gear's abilities.

How spec works:

- Mastery unlocks gear use and gives broad progress in a weapon or armor tree.
- Specialization adds more power to specific item lines.
- Fame farming builds spec, which makes cheaper sets perform better over time.
- High spec does not replace knowing your role. A high-IP player wasting
  defensives or damage windows can still lose fights.

Practical advice:

- If an LFG has a minimum IP, treat it as a floor. Do not join under the floor
  unless the caller approves it.
- Bring the required weapon, armor, cape, potion, food, mount, and swaps.
- Repair before content and check durability after deaths.
- Do not over-invest in a lethal set you cannot afford to replace.

How to answer "what should I level":

- Ask what content they want: faction, ganking, roads, small scale, ZvZ, PvE, or
  gathering.
- Suggest picking one main role first: healer, tank/catch, support, ranged DPS,
  melee DPS, or economy/gathering.
- Tell them to follow guild comps and caller requests instead of chasing every
  new meta build.

Bot caution:

Do not invent exact current meta numbers or patch-specific item stats. For
current build details, point members to the event comp, guild comp library, or a
caller.
