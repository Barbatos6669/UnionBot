# Albion New Player To Guild Member Path

Keywords: albion new player beginner onboarding guild member learning path tutorial destiny board fame learning points registration lfg role gear ip content discord voice economy lethal safe yellow red black

Use this file when a new member asks what to do, how to start, why Discord
matters, how to prepare for guild content, or how to move from solo play into
organized group content.

First hour:

- Finish the tutorial and learn movement, gathering, crafting, market, combat,
  and Destiny Board basics.
- Do not spend silver trying to look rich. Buy gear you can replace.
- Pick one simple weapon line and one armor direction long enough to learn it.
- Register in Discord so the guild can connect Discord identity to Albion
  identity and assign the right roles.

First day:

- Learn what zone colors mean before entering red/black areas.
- Try safe or yellow content to learn buttons, but understand that lethal
  content feels different.
- Join voice for guild content even if you do not talk much. Listening matters.
- Read LFG posts before signing up. Check timer, content type, IP minimum, comp,
  food, potion, mount, and form-up instructions.

First week:

- Build one affordable lethal set that fits guild content.
- Learn one role well: tank/catch, healer, support, ranged DPS, melee DPS,
  gatherer, scout, or transport.
- Ask for comp help before buying expensive gear.
- Do faction, roads, ganking, group dungeons, gathering, or market work based on
  what content roles you want.
- Bank loot often. Repair often. Do not transport everything at once.

Moving into lethal content:

- Bring gear you can afford to lose.
- Use the event's requested build, not a random solo build.
- Stay with group. Do not chase unless the caller says chase.
- Listen for hold damage, engage, defensive, reset, mount up, and bank calls.
- If you die, ask what caused it: position, cooldowns, dismount, cleanse, mount,
  wrong gear, wrong timing, or bad information.

Discord habits:

- Register first.
- Pick content ping roles so the right LFGs notify you.
- Use LFG signups instead of "anyone doing anything?" when possible.
- Ask in the correct channel. Registration, LFG, market, SSO routes, regear,
  bounties, faction, and content roles each have a purpose.
- Do not expect officers to read every random chat message while in game. Use
  the bot workflows when they exist.

Common new-player warnings:

- IP is not everything. A bad role in high IP is still bad.
- Gathering gear helps gathering, not fighting.
- Cheap, correct, replaceable gear is better than expensive gear you are afraid
  to use.
- Do not bring transport loads into red/black danger without route planning.
- Do not trust instant market prices in arbitrage posts. Use buy/sell orders.
- If a caller gives a comp, follow it or ask before signing.

UnionBot answer style:

- Give new players the next action, not an essay.
- For registration, route to the configured registration channel and explain the
  button/screenshot flow.
- For content, route to event board/LFG and content roles.
- For gear, ask what content and role before recommending anything.
