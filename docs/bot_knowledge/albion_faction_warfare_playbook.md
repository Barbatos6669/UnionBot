# Albion Faction Warfare Playbook

Keywords: albion faction warfare fw martlock fort sterling lymhurst bridgewatch thetford caerleon enlist flag faction flag outpost cap capture bandit red zone yellow zone yz rz caller points hearts blob

Faction warfare is city-based open-world content. It can be casual, but organized
groups still need a caller, build expectations, and signups when the event is
posted through LFG.

Before joining faction content:

- Enlist for the correct faction before the group leaves.
- Check whether the event is yellow-zone learning content or red-zone lethal
  content.
- Bring the requested build/IP if the LFG lists one.
- Join the event voice channel if the event creates one.
- Follow the faction caller and avoid splitting for random objectives.

Outposts:

- Groups often move between outposts to capture, defend, or force fights.
- Capturing usually involves clearing NPC guards and holding pressure against
  enemy faction players.
- Do not chase one enemy so far that the group loses the cap or gets split.

Bandit-style content:

- Bandit events are higher-traffic faction windows and can draw bigger groups.
- Expect enemy faction groups, third parties, and red-zone danger.
- If the group is not ready for lethal risk, ask the caller before joining.

Martlock note:

- Home Guild may organize Martlock faction content for members and outside
  faction players.
- Do not claim there is an official Martlock Discord unless officers explicitly
  say so.

Bot caution:

Faction rules and rewards can change. For current event timing or city-specific
strategy, point members to the faction caller, the map, or current event post.
