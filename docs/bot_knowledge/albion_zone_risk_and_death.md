# Albion Zone Risk And Death Rules

Keywords: albion zone zones blue yellow red black yz rz bz death knocked down full loot lethal nonlethal flag flagged hostile reputation cluster dungeon road mist faction

Albion risk is mostly controlled by zone type and flagging state. When unsure,
tell members to check the zone color and the event LFG before bringing expensive
gear.

Zone basics:

- Blue zones are safest. Normal hostile player full-loot PvP is not the focus
  there.
- Yellow zones are nonlethal for normal open-world PvP. Players can be knocked
  down and lose durability, but they usually do not drop their inventory from
  normal yellow-zone PvP.
- Red zones are lethal PvP zones. Hostile players can kill you and your gear can
  drop. Reputation and hostile flagging matter in red zones.
- Black zones are lethal PvP zones with full-loot risk and no reputation safety
  net. Treat black zone as real loss content.
- Avalonian Roads, Mists, special dungeons, faction flagging, or event-specific
  rules can change the practical risk. If a member is unsure, tell them to ask
  the caller before stepping out.

How to explain death:

- Knocked down usually means durability loss and a wait, not full loot.
- Full-loot death means other players can loot dropped gear and inventory.
- Trash chance means some items may be destroyed instead of dropped.
- Silver carried as currency is not the same as items in a bag, but transport
  goods, gear, loot, journals, resources, and market items can be lost in lethal
  content.

Faction warning:

- Faction flagging can make players valid targets for enemy faction players.
- Do not tell players a faction event is safe just because it starts near a
  royal city. Red-zone faction content is still risky.

Bot caution:

Rules can change with patches and special event rules. If the question is about
an exact edge case, tell the member to check the in-game zone warning, current
patch notes, or an officer/caller.
