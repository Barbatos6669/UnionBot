# Albion Open World Survival

Keywords: albion open world survival survive gank ganking escape mount dismount bubble invisibility shrine portal road black zone red zone rz bz scout inspect nametag blob offscreen choke cooldown purge boots defensives

Most open-world deaths happen before the fight truly starts. Survival is about
route choice, mount discipline, cooldown discipline, and not letting enemies
choose the fight for free.

Before leaving town:

- Bring gear you can afford to lose.
- Bring the right mount for the job. Transport, gathering, and roaming need
  different mounts.
- Bring food, potion, cape, and escape tools if your role needs them.
- Check the route and know the next safe point.
- If transporting, split risk instead of carrying the entire bank in one trip.

Travel habits:

- Do not autopilot through chokes, roads, bridges, exits, or obvious dismount
  spots.
- Keep your mount healthy. If your mount is low, leave the risky area.
- Watch nametags, blobs, corpses, mobs being killed, and players standing too
  still near roads or gates.
- Inspect suspicious players when possible, but do not stop in danger just to
  inspect.
- Use invisibility/bubble mechanics intentionally. Do not waste them by clicking
  around without a plan.

If chased:

- Do not panic-use every defensive at once.
- Save boots, cleanse, invisibility, or defensive skills for the moment they
  actually stop catch.
- Avoid running deeper into enemy territory unless it is the only route.
- If you are in comms, call where you are, what is chasing, and what exit you
  are taking.

If fighting:

- Stay near your healer and group unless the caller tells you to split.
- Do not tunnel one low target if it drags you into a bad choke or away from
  heals.
- Reset means stop chasing and regroup.

Bot caution:

Do not promise safety. Albion open world is dynamic, and a route that was clear
five minutes ago can become dangerous.
