# Albion PvP And Comps

Keywords: pvp comp composition shotcaller caller engage catch pierce purge burst healer support tank clap melt ip gear defensives rotation focus fire

PvP wins usually come from timing, roles, and discipline, not only high IP.

Basic kill window:

catch -> force defensives or purge -> pierce/debuff -> damage buff -> burst -> execute -> reset

Common group roles:

- Caller / shotcaller: chooses fights, calls engages, resets, and target focus.
- Tank / catch: stops enemies from leaving and starts the kill window.
- Pierce / debuff: lowers enemy defenses so damage matters.
- Burst DPS: saves damage for the called window.
- Healer: keeps the group alive and manages cleanse/defensive timing.
- Support: purges, defensives, utility, swaps, or extra control depending on comp.

Important habits:

- Hold damage until the caller calls the dump.
- Do not randomly blow purge, boots, defensive, or big damage before the window.
- If the target uses Cleric Robe, Hunter Hood, Merc Jacket, resist pot, boots,
  or cleanse, call it and adjust.
- Do not tunnel so hard that the group outruns heals or loses formation.
- Reset means stop chasing, regroup, heal, and prepare the next rotation.

IP guidance:

- Minimum IP on an LFG is a floor, not a guarantee of success.
- Weapon IP/spec matters a lot for damage and healing.
- Armor choice changes damage, defense, and role. Cloth is more offensive,
  plate is more defensive, leather is usually between them.

Bot caution:

Do not invent current meta comps. If a member asks for the current best build,
point them to the event comp, guild comp library, or an experienced caller.
