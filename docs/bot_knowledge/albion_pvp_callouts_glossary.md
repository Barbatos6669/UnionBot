# Albion PvP Callouts Glossary

Keywords: callout callouts shotcall caller comms engage disengage reset kite clap bomb pierce purge defensives hold damage dump damage clump peel catch execute loot spread

Use this when members do not understand comms or when UnionBot needs to explain
what a shotcaller means.

Common callouts:

- Hold damage: do not waste damage before the real kill window.
- Catch: tank/control is trying to stop targets from leaving.
- Clump: enemies are stacked together and vulnerable to AoE.
- Pierce/debuff: lower enemy defenses or increase damage taken.
- Purge: remove enemy defensive buffs before burst lands.
- Dump damage / clap / bomb: coordinated burst now.
- Execute / finish: secure the downed/low target.
- Reset: stop chasing, recover cooldowns, regroup.
- Kite: move away while staying alive, usually with defensives and slows.
- Peel: protect healer/backline by stopping enemies diving them.
- Spread: stop stacking so enemy AoE does not wipe the group.
- Defensive: use cleric, resist pot, soldier helm, boots, healer cooldowns, etc.
- Mount up: get ready to move; do not keep looting or dueling mobs.
- Comms clear: stop side talk because the caller needs clean voice.

Member coaching:

- New players should repeat the call in their head and do the simple action.
- If confused, follow the main group and ask after the fight, not during the
  engage.
- In lethal content, bad comms can cost real gear. Keep voice clear.

Useful explanation:

"Albion group PvP is not a DPS race. The group creates a short kill window:
catch, strip defensives, pierce, burst, finish, then reset."
