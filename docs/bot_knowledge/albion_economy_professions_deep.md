# Albion Economy And Professions Deep Reference

Keywords: albion economy market marketplace black market buy order sell order arbitrage gathering resource refining crafting focus resource return rate rrr local production bonus city bonus island farming laborer journal transport hauling weight burden mount resource mob enchanted resources biome

Use this file when members ask how to make silver, how buy/sell orders work,
whether a route is risky, what gathering/refining/crafting mean, or how guild
resource missions should be explained.

Economic chain:

- Gathering creates raw resources.
- Refining turns raw resources into usable materials.
- Crafting turns materials into gear, consumables, tools, furniture, and other
  player-used goods.
- Hauling moves goods between cities, hideouts, islands, markets, and danger
  zones.
- Markets convert time, risk, and knowledge into silver.

Gathering:

- Gathering uses tools and harvests resource nodes in blue, yellow, red, and
  black zones.
- Main resource families include fiber, hide, ore, stone, wood, and fishing.
- Enchanted resources are more valuable and can produce stronger crafted goods.
- Gathering gear gives yield and escape/survival value, but it is not a normal
  fighting set.
- Resource mobs can be killed and harvested, often faster than ordinary nodes.
- Biomes influence which resources are common in a zone.

Refining:

- Refining converts raw resources into materials used by crafting.
- Profit depends on resource prices, refining fees/taxes, return rate, city
  bonus, focus use, transport cost, and sell price.
- Refiners should usually compare buy order cost, refining return, fee, and
  expected sell order value rather than buying instantly at market ask.

Crafting:

- Crafting is the final step after gathering/farming and refining/pastures.
- Crafting Focus can improve resource return and is premium-limited over time.
- City/local production bonuses can make one city better for a profession than
  another.
- Crafting profit is not guaranteed. It depends on inputs, return rate, focus,
  taxes, item demand, quality, market depth, and time to sell.

Resource return rate:

- Resource Return Rate gives back part of the materials used in refining or
  crafting.
- Local Production Bonus feeds into return rate. Higher bonus means more
  returned resources, but exact formulas should be verified when calculating
  profit.
- Hideouts and city bonuses can change the math. Do not give exact silver
  profit unless using current market data.

Journals and laborers:

- Journals can be filled by activities like gathering, crafting, or combat,
  depending on journal type.
- Laborers convert filled journals into resource rewards over time.
- Journal profit depends on book cost, house/laborer setup, trophy/furniture
  happiness, market price, and logistics.

Markets and arbitrage:

- Buy order means you are offering to buy at your price and waiting for sellers.
- Sell order means you list at your target price and wait for buyers.
- Instant buy/sell is convenience and often worse profit.
- Arbitrage posts should be read as trade ideas: place a buy order near the
  suggested buy price, wait for fill, haul safely, then place a sell order near
  the suggested sell price.
- The bot must not promise that suggested prices will still exist when someone
  arrives.

Transport and risk:

- Weight, mount choice, route, zone type, time of day, and enemy activity decide
  transport risk.
- Safe city to safe city through blue/yellow routes is lower risk.
- Routes touching red zones, black zones, Caerleon, Roads, or black-market
  hauling should be treated as risky.
- Split loads, avoid overloading, use appropriate mounts, scout dangerous zones,
  bank often, and do not haul what the guild cannot afford to lose.

UnionBot answer style:

- If asked "how do I make silver?", ask whether they prefer gathering, refining,
  crafting, transport, market flipping, PvE, PvP loot, or guild missions.
- If asked "is this arbitrage profitable?", say it is a suggestion based on
  available data, not a guarantee. Mention buy orders and sell orders.
- If asked "what should I gather for the guild?", point to current guild
  resource missions or bounties if configured.
