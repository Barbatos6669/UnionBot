# Albion T6 Avalonian Dungeon Planning

This is Home Guild guidance for preparing a Tier 6 Avalonian Dungeon. Treat
it as event-planning doctrine, not permanent patch law. If the event post or
shotcaller says something different, follow the event post.

## Short Answer

The safest default T6 Avalonian Dungeon shell is a controlled core-role shell:

- Main tank: 1H Hammer + Leering Cane.
- Off-tank: Incubus Mace + Sarcophagus.
- Main healer: Ironroot Staff + Celestial Censer.
- Holy healer: 1H Holy Staff + Celestial Censer.
- Party healer: Ironroot Staff + Mistcaller.
- Great Arcane support.
- 1H Arcane silence/control support.
- Ironroot BMS utility slot.
- DPS/flex: Blazing, Lightcaller, Mistpiercer, Badon/Longbow/Skystrider,
  Light Crossbow/Weeping/Siege, and Great Frost.

For home guild, T6 should be treated as a learning and consistency tier. The goal is a
clean full clear, clean comms, controlled pulls, and safe extraction. Do not sell
T6 Ava as "bring whatever."

## Which Shell To Use

Current full-clear core:

- 1H Hammer, Incubus, Ironroot main healer, Holy healer, Ironroot party healer,
  Great Arcane, 1H Arcane, Ironroot BMS, Blazing, Lightcaller, Mistpiercer, then
  caller-approved ranged DPS flex.
- Best default if the roster is organized and the support/healer players can
  keep clean defensive, silence, and reset windows.

Healer fallback:

- Ironroot Staff + Celestial Censer is the default main healer line for this
  comp.
- Holy stays in the group as 1H Holy Staff + Celestial Censer for full clears.
- Hallowfall is an approved Holy swap, not the default.
- Stillgaze Staff is not a healing staff; do not assign it as main healer.
- If a caller wants a Stillgaze or other shapeshifter slot, treat it as a
  caller-approved DPS/support flex, not a healer replacement.

Lean shell:

- Hammer, Incubus, Ironroot main healer, Holy healer, party healer or BMS
  support, Great Arcane or 1H Arcane, and enough ranged DPS to clear safely.
- Only use with experienced players and clean comms. Do not cut the main tank,
  off-tank, or main healer.

Large-roster progression shell:

- Full core plus 3-8 flex ranged DPS from Badon, Longbow, Skystrider, Light
  Crossbow, Weeping, Siege Bow, Great Frost, second Mistpiercer, or second
  Lightcaller.
- Best for teaching T6 mechanics to 15-20 players.

## IP Bands

These are guild planning bands, not official game caps:

- Learning floor: 15-20 organized players, roughly 1180-1220 average IP. Tanks
  and main healer should still lean higher, roughly 1250-1300.
- Comfort band: 12-15 players, roughly 1220-1280 average IP. Prefer 1300+ on
  tank, main healer, Holy healer, key support roles, and DPS weapon/core pieces.
- Lean burst band: 10-12 experienced players, roughly 1275-1350 average IP.
  Tank, main healer, and key support/DPS should be among the highest-IP players.

Practical rule: use T8-flat as the reference, then improve weapon and chest
first if a player is short on spec. Do not let a low-IP tank, healer, or
core support carry a lean roster.

## Pull And Comms Discipline

Most T6 wipes come from bad clumps, early damage, panic healing, and comm clutter.
Use a simple call sequence:

1. Outer scout confirms entrance/route status.
2. Caller marks the pull and checks swaps.
3. Main tank gathers and line-of-sights the stack.
4. Off-tank compresses or controls.
5. Arcane/support window lands.
6. Healers stabilize the tank through the aggro spike.
7. Caller says damage.
8. DPS bursts together.
9. Reset cooldowns, loot, scout check, then move.

Short call language:

- "Pulling."
- "Stacked."
- "Pierce."
- "Damage."
- "Personals."
- "Reset."

Only the caller owns pull comms. Tank speaks when the clump breaks or the boss
must move. Healers speak for emergencies or mana.

## Boss Notes

Knight Captain:

- Play behind the boss.
- Reposition after jumps or reflect transitions.
- Save defensives for the major explosion window.
- Do not rely on old forced-movement or reflect-abuse habits.

Dancing Queen / Archmage:

- Spread properly.
- Rotate defensive abilities during the pizza/laser pattern.
- Do not hard-code old movement patterns; direction can be less predictable.

Construct:

- Pin near a wall or alcove when possible.
- Move only when damage stacks rise so falling rocks stay controlled.
- Many callers move around roughly 6-8 stacks, but follow the current caller.

High Priestess:

- Treat add waves as the main mechanic.
- Do not let spawned mobs cluster and heal each other.
- Use the golden circle/healing mechanic correctly if called.

Basilisk:

- Stay spread.
- Use defensives on explosions.
- This is usually more discipline check than comp check.

Sir Bedivere:

- Final discipline check.
- Stay behind the boss.
- Dodge red telegraphs.
- Do not build a strategy around old exploit-era reflect behavior.

## Scouting And Safety Checklist

Before clicking a T6 Ava map:

- Outer scout assigned before map click.
- Rear info/scout slot assigned for smaller rosters or high-risk routes.
- Role swaps called before each boss door.
- Potion assignments confirmed.
- Loot and split rules locked before entry.
- Mount discipline set.
- Caller owns pull comms.
- Extraction plan agreed before bags are full.

Ava dungeon groups are visible and valuable targets. Treat the raid as PvE with
PvP risk, not safe instanced fame.

## First-Time T6 Ava Tutorial

Use this tutorial when a member asks how to prepare for or run their first T6
Avalonian Dungeon. Keep the answer calm and step-by-step.

### 1. Before The Event

The caller should post an LFG early enough for people to gear correctly.

Required event information:

- Tier and objective: T6 Ava full clear, learning run, or chest-focused run.
- Start time and form-up time.
- Minimum IP and any stricter IP expectations for tank, main healer, key
  supports, and DPS weapon/core pieces.
- Attached comp: use `T6 Ava Full Clear` from the comp library.
- Loot split rule.
- Voice requirement.
- Who owns the map.
- Who is outer scout and who watches rear/route info.

If the roster is mostly newer players, call it a learning/progression run. Do
not advertise it as a fast full clear.

### 2. Roster Check

Before leaving town, the caller or officer should check:

- Main tank is on 1H Hammer and understands pull control.
- Off-tank is on Incubus and understands compress/control timing.
- Main healer is on Ironroot Staff + Celestial Censer.
- Holy healer is on 1H Holy Staff + Celestial Censer, or a caller-approved
  Hallowfall swap.
- Party healer, Great Arcane, 1H Arcane, and Ironroot BMS are covered before
  filling extra DPS.
- DPS are mostly ranged and following the comp.
- Scouts are assigned and know they are not optional.
- Everyone has food, potion, repair silver, and a mount.

If key roles are missing, downgrade the goal. A messy T6 Ava learning run is
better than pretending a weak roster is ready for clean full clear pacing.

### 3. Voice Setup

Before the first pull, the caller should say:

```text
Only caller talks during pulls.
Tank speaks if the clump breaks or boss must move.
Healers speak for mana or emergency only.
Everyone else keeps comms clear unless you see danger.
Do not hit early. Wait for the damage call.
```

New players should be told that clean comms are part of the mechanic. Ava wipes
often come from confusion, not just low IP.

### 4. Entry And Scouting

Before clicking in:

- Outer scout checks the entrance area.
- Group confirms the route is not stale.
- Caller confirms everyone is mounted and ready.
- Loot/split rules are repeated once.
- The group enters together.

Inside, do not sprint ahead. The tank leads. The scout/caller decides when the
group moves, pauses, or backs out.

### 5. Basic Trash Pull Tutorial

The simple pull flow is:

1. Tank moves first and gathers mobs.
2. Group waits at safe range.
3. Tank line-of-sights or positions the pack.
4. Off-tank compresses or controls if needed.
5. Arcane/support window lands.
6. Healer stabilizes the tank.
7. Caller says "damage."
8. DPS dumps together.
9. Group resets cooldowns before the next pull.

The most important beginner rule: do not hit before the caller says damage.
Early damage can split aggro, break clumps, trigger panic defensives, and make
the real damage window worse.

### 6. Boss Door Routine

At every boss door:

- Stop the group.
- Explain the boss in one or two sentences.
- Confirm swaps if any.
- Confirm defensive order if the boss needs it.
- Remind players where to stand.
- Pull only after comms are quiet.

Good caller example:

```text
This boss is a spacing check. Stay behind boss, dodge red, save defensives for
the big explosion. Do not greed casts. If you are confused, follow the group.
Pulling in 3, 2, 1.
```

### 7. If Someone Dies

Do not let one death turn into five deaths.

- Caller says whether to reset, continue, or hold.
- Healers do not chase a dead player into danger.
- DPS stop panic-comming and wait for the call.
- If the death happened because of a mechanic, explain it once after the pull.

Keep the tone calm. T6 Ava is partly for learning.

### 8. Loot And Extraction

Before bags are full:

- Caller checks scout info.
- Group banks or exits before greed becomes dangerous.
- Nobody leaves alone unless explicitly told.
- Loot split handler records what needs to be recorded.

The run is not successful only because a boss died. The run is successful when
the group extracts value, learns mechanics, and does not throw gear away for no
reason.

### 9. After The Run

After the dungeon:

- Close the LFG/event if the bot needs it.
- Confirm attendance if required.
- Handle loot split.
- Note what roles were missing.
- Note which boss or pull caused problems.
- Update the comp or guide if the guild learned something useful.

Short after-action prompt:

```text
What went well?
What caused deaths or slowdowns?
What role/build were we missing?
What should we change before the next T6 Ava?
```
