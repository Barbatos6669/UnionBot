# Albion Dungeon And PvP Instance Reference

Keywords: dungeon dungeons corrupted cd hellgate hellgates random dungeon expedition hce static solo group pve pvp lethal non lethal infamy invade boss chest ip cap

Use this when members ask what kind of dungeon/content they should run.

Corrupted Dungeons:

- Solo PvPvE instance.
- Player clears corrupted mobs to progress and can be invaded for a 1v1.
- Hunter is the learning/non-lethal style; Stalker and Slayer are lethal/full
  loot styles. Slayer has higher entry expectations and infamy requirements.
- If the player hates 1v1 PvP, corrupted dungeons may feel stressful.

Hellgates:

- Team PvPvE dungeon format.
- Common formats are 2v2 and 5v5; some guides also reference 10v10.
- Teams clear demons and can be forced into fights as the area shrinks.
- Non-lethal variants are good practice. Lethal variants are full-loot.
- Good comps usually need clear roles, not random damage builds.

Random dungeons:

- Hidden entrances in the open world.
- Solo and group versions exist.
- Risk depends on the zone and whether enemies can dive the group.
- Higher-risk zones generally mean better fame/loot potential.

Expeditions / HCE:

- Instanced PvE from cities. Normal expeditions are low PvP risk.
- Hardcore Expeditions require proper comp, maps, and coordinated PvE play.
- This is not the same as open-world fame farming or black-zone static content.

Statics and world boss zones:

- Open-world PvE zones that can attract hostile PvP groups.
- Require scouting, caller awareness, and escape planning when lethal.

UnionBot answer rule:

- Ask group size, risk tolerance, and whether they want PvP or fame.
- If they are new, suggest non-lethal practice before lethal full-loot content.
- If they want guild content, point them to the event board/LFG.
