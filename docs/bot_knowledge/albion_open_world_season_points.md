# Albion Open World Season Points Guide

Keywords: season points might favor conqueror challenge open world black zone bz roaming castle outpost territory power core energy vortex world boss roads mists corrupted dungeon hellgate crystal spider gathering score points guild challenge

Use this when members ask how to help Home Guild score points, build guild
season progress, or find useful open-world content by group size.

Core idea:

- Do risky Albion content together, especially in black zones, Roads, Mists,
  and other lethal areas.
- Personal activity can generate Might and Favor through the Conqueror's
  Challenge system.
- Guild progress also comes from guild objectives such as Power Cores, Energy
  Vortices, territories, castles, castle outposts, world bosses, and similar
  season systems.
- Exact values, timers, and point math are patch-sensitive. Tell members to use
  the current in-game tooltip, map timer, event post, or caller notes when an
  exact value matters.

How members should think about it:

- Solo members can still help. They should do lethal Mists, corrupted dungeons,
  black-zone or Roads mobs, gathering, scouting, and safe banking runs.
- Small teams create the most everyday value. They can roam, clear Roads
  chests, kill crystal spiders, escort gatherers, fight over coffers, gank,
  defend guild gatherers, and pick up smaller cores when safe.
- Medium teams should look for objectives. They can run statics, dive groups,
  contest Roads objectives, escort Power Cores and Energy Vortices, take weaker
  castle outposts, and pressure nearby maps.
- Large teams should form around callers. They can contest castles, major
  outposts, territories, world bosses, large cores/vortexes, hideout defense,
  and ZvZ-style objectives.

Solo options:

- Lethal Mists: camps, wisps, coffers, spiders, gathering, and opportunistic
  PvP.
- Corrupted dungeons: 1v1 PvP/PvE with infamy and loot progression.
- Black-zone or Roads open-world mobs: upgraded mobs are good fame/silver and
  teach movement.
- Gathering in black zone, Roads, or Mists: slower point value but important
  guild economy value.
- Scouting: find enemy groups, cores, vortexes, chests, spiders, paths, and
  safe exits. Good scouts create content for everyone else.

Small team options, 2-5:

- Black-zone roaming: fame, kills, coffers, treasure, and objective scouting.
- Roads green/blue chests depending on comp and risk.
- Crystal spiders and open-world mini-objectives.
- Duo/small Mists, 2v2 or 5v5 Hellgates, and dungeon dives.
- Gatherer escort or anti-gank patrols.
- Small Power Core grabs when the map is quiet.

Medium team options, 6-12:

- Static dungeon fame farms or dives.
- Roads blue/gold chests with healer/support.
- Energy Vortex and Power Core escorts.
- Castle outpost attempts when opposition is low.
- Territory pressure, mage/energy raids if current season mechanics support it,
  and defensive response groups.
- Small-scale fights where the group has a real comp: caller, healer, support,
  catch, DPS, and reset plan.

Large team options, 13+:

- Castle and castle outpost timers.
- Territory fights and territory defenses.
- World boss/raid-zone objectives.
- Large Energy Vortex or Power Core contests.
- Hideout defense and placement support.
- ZvZ CTAs, alliance objectives, and high-risk map control.

Union guidance:

- Post or join an LFG instead of waiting in chat.
- Group up when possible. Five members doing a plan together usually helps more
  than five people playing alone.
- Bring gear you can afford to lose.
- Bank often. Dead players with full bags donate progress to someone else.
- Favor good fights and clean objectives over random inting.
- New players should join learning-safe groups first; experienced members
  should lead, scout, teach, and help build confidence.
