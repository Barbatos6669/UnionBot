# Albion Terms Glossary

Keywords: albion glossary terms slang focus fire overcharge oc disarray bubble outlaw cluster queue ip spec softcap hardcap rrr focus crafting focus fame credit learning points lp clap bomb kite peel reset engage purge pierce cleanse defensives rat gank dismount execute loot split trash rate road portal shrine invisibility

Use this file for short Discord questions like "what is oc?", "what is focus
fire?", "what is disarray?", "what is a bubble?", or "what does RRR mean?"

Combat and PvP terms:

- Focus fire: the group attacks the same called target at the same time. In
  Albion, focus fire also interacts with Focus Fire/Resilience mechanics that
  reduce dogpile damage when many players hit one target.
- Target call: the caller names or marks who should die. Damage should follow
  the call instead of scattering across random targets.
- Resilience: protection against many attackers dogpiling one target. This is
  why random damage from everyone is weaker than a clean damage window.
- Resilience Penetration: a stat/weapon property that ignores part of the
  anti-dogpile protection.
- Kill window: the short moment when catch, purge, pierce, damage buffs, burst,
  and execute line up.
- Clap: coordinated burst damage, usually AoE, meant to delete a clump.
- Bomb: similar to clap; often means an explosive engage on a stacked enemy.
- Engage: the call to start the fight or commit the first catch.
- Reset: stop chasing, stabilize, recover cooldowns, and regroup.
- Kite: move away while surviving and dealing pressure when possible.
- Peel: protect an ally by slowing, stunning, knocking back, cleansing, or
  pressuring enemies off them.
- Purge: remove an enemy buff or defensive.
- Pierce: lower enemy defenses so follow-up damage hits harder.
- Cleanse: remove or prevent crowd control effects.
- Defensives: abilities or consumables used to survive a damage window, such as
  cleric robe, soldier helm, resistance potion, gigantify potion, boots, etc.
- Dismount: force someone off their mount so they can be killed or caught.
- Execute: finish a low-health target or use a finisher ability.
- Rat: a player trying to take loot/objectives opportunistically without taking
  a fair fight.
- I-frame / immunity: brief immunity or untargetable timing from specific
  abilities. Exact behavior depends on the skill tooltip.
- Reflect: damage returned to attackers. Stop hitting into reflect unless the
  caller says it is safe.
- Bait: looking vulnerable to force enemy cooldowns or movement.
- Trade: exchanging cooldowns, HP, position, objectives, or loot pressure.

Large fight terms:

- ZvZ: zerg vs zerg, large group PvP.
- Disarray: large-fight debuff/scaling mechanic in Outlands fights. Bigger
  groups can suffer penalties, so more people is not always automatically
  better.
- Smart Cluster Queue: zone entry system for overcrowded clusters. It can stop
  everyone from entering and affects large objective fights.
- Blob: large group indicator or large visible enemy force, depending on context.
- Clump: stacked enemy players. Good clumps are valuable because AoE damage and
  CC get more value.
- Bomb squad: players assigned to deliver coordinated AoE burst onto clumps.
- Defensive tank: tank/support role focused on protecting allies rather than
  only engaging enemies.
- Frontline: tanks/bruisers/supports holding space near the enemy.
- Backline: healers/ranged/supports who must stay safe while contributing.

Gear and progression:

- IP: Item Power. It is gear strength, affected by tier, enchantment, quality,
  mastery, specialization, and other systems.
- Spec: specialization in a weapon/armor line. Higher spec increases strength
  and makes cheaper gear perform better.
- Softcap: effective IP above the cap is reduced, not fully ignored.
- Hardcap: effective IP above the cap is not counted.
- OC: overcharge. Temporarily boosts gear power but costs energy and can damage
  or trash items afterward. Use it when the caller says to.
- Swap: extra gear piece carried for a planned situation.
- Trash rate: chance that items are destroyed on death instead of dropping.
- Repair: restoring durability. Repair before content.
- IP floor: minimum Item Power required by the LFG/caller.
- Regear: guild replacement/support process for approved losses, depending on
  policy and content.

Economy and crafting:

- Focus: premium resource used mostly for crafting/refining/farming efficiency.
  It is not the same as focus fire.
- RRR: Resource Return Rate. The percent of resources returned when crafting or
  refining.
- LP: Learning Points. Used to speed unlocks after a fame threshold.
- Fame Credits: combat fame overflow that can be spent into other combat nodes.
- Buy order: offer to buy at your price and wait for sellers.
- Sell order: list at your target price and wait for buyers.
- Instant buy/sell: faster but usually worse profit.
- Spread: gap between buy and sell prices.
- Tax/setup fee: market costs that must be included in profit math.
- Margin: expected profit after item cost, taxes, focus, hauling risk, and time.
- Black Market: Caerleon market where items are bought for loot-table supply.
  Transporting to it is risky because Caerleon routes can involve red-zone risk.

Open-world survival:

- Bubble: temporary protection/invisibility-type safety after zoning, respawn,
  or certain entry situations. Do not break it early unless you mean to move or
  fight.
- Shrine/invis shrine: open-world invisibility source used for safer travel.
- Outlaw: status/restriction from hostile activity that affects zoning and safe
  access.
- Portal charges: Roads/portal availability can limit how many players enter.
- Bank often: deposit loot before greed turns profit into a death mail.
- Scout: player checking route/enemy movement ahead of the group.
- Naked scout: cheap/no-risk scout character or set used to gather information.
- Choke: narrow path where enemies can catch or bomb a group.
- Mount timer/dismount timer: timing around being mounted, dismounted, or
  unable to instantly use abilities.

UnionBot answer style:

- Keep glossary answers short first, then offer one practical example.
- If a term has two meanings, separate them. Example: focus fire is combat;
  crafting focus is economy.
- Do not turn a one-term question into a full beginner guide unless the member
  asks for more detail.
