# Albion Faction Warfare After Realm Divided

Keywords: faction warfare martlock realm divided province provinces fortress fortresses faction battle standard battle standards banner bandit assault outpost camp chest supplies faction transport red zone yellow zone caller

Use this when members ask about Martlock faction warfare, bandit, standards,
outposts, fortresses, faction roles, or why the home guild is organizing faction content.

Core idea:

Faction warfare is city-vs-city open-world content. Home Guild is
supporting Martlock faction activity because there is no single official
Martlock Discord strong enough to organize every public player. Our Discord can
give faction players cleaner LFG, voice, roles, and caller structure.

Modern faction warfare:

- Players enlist with a city faction such as Martlock, Bridgewatch, Lymhurst,
  Fort Sterling, Thetford, or Caerleon.
- Players earn faction standing/points through faction objectives, fighting
  enemy faction players, capturing/defending objectives, and related content.
- Realm Divided changed faction warfare into a more active Royal Continent
  campaign with clearer objectives such as provinces, fortresses, camps, chests,
  transports, and front-line movement.
- Realm Divided Part II added more dynamic objective play, including battle
  standards/banner-style gameplay and a reworked Bandit Assault flow.

How to answer members:

- "Enlist for Martlock before the event starts."
- "Read the LFG: yellow-zone faction is learning/practice; red-zone faction can
  be lethal and needs real gear discipline."
- "Follow the faction caller. Do not split for random caps while the group is
  moving."
- "If the event creates a faction voice channel, join that channel so access and
  comms stay clean."
- "Bring the requested IP and build. Faction content gets messy fast when people
  bring random solo sets."

Faction group basics:

- Small faction party: caller, healer, pierce/support, bruisers, ranged pressure.
- Public zerg: caller discipline matters more than individual plays.
- Battle standards / banner-style objectives reward escort, peel, and regroup
  discipline. Do not chase kills away from the objective unless the caller says.
- Bandit-style events need clear rally, regroup, and disengage calls.
