# Albion Economy, Crafting, And Refining

Keywords: albion economy crafting craft crafter refining refine refiner focus premium resource return rate rrr city bonus buy order sell order market tax setup fee journal laborer island transport profit spreadsheet

Albion economy is a trading and production game. Profit comes from planning,
not from assuming instant market prices.

Core ideas:

- Buy orders are how traders try to acquire items below instant buy price.
- Sell orders are how traders try to sell above instant sell price.
- Taxes, setup fees, crafting fees, transport risk, and time must be included.
- Focus can improve profit, especially with premium and the right activity.
- City bonuses and return rates matter, but exact values can change.

Crafting/refining checklist:

- Know the input cost.
- Know the expected output value.
- Include taxes and station fees.
- Include focus cost if used.
- Include transport cost/risk if moving resources.
- Do not craft large batches without checking market demand.

Guild economy advice:

- Protect coffers. Do not pay rewards that cost more than the value generated.
- For guild assets, officers should approve risky transports or large spending.
- Track debts, payouts, and confirmed payments through the bot when possible.

Bot caution:

Do not promise profit. Market prices move and orders may not fill.
