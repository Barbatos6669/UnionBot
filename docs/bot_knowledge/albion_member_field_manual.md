# Albion Member Field Manual

Use this file for normal member questions where they need a practical answer,
not a lecture. Keep answers short, useful, and honest about uncertainty.

## Fast Answer Rules

- If a member asks for exact patch values, exact silver profit, exact item stats,
  or exact drop rates, tell them to verify in the current in-game UI, event post,
  or with the caller. Albion changes too often to promise exact numbers forever.
- If a member asks what to bring to guild content, trust the event post and comp
  first. Generic advice is second.
- If a member asks if content is safe, answer by zone and death rule. Red, black,
  Roads, lethal Mists, lethal Hellgates, and most Outlands content should be
  treated as gear-loss content.
- If a member is new, route them toward cheap gear, voice, signups, and learning
  one role. Do not tell them to buy expensive sets before they understand the
  content.

## What Should I Do Today?

Ask three things:

1. How many people are available?
2. Are they trying to make silver, fame, season points, or practice PvP?
3. Are they willing to risk gear?

Solo:

- Safe economy: gathering, refining math, crafting focus, market buy/sell
  orders, faction transport inside safe routes.
- Learning PvP: yellow-zone faction, arena, non-lethal corrupted, cheap Mists.
- Lethal growth: black-zone solo roaming, gathering, tracking, ratting objectives,
  lethal Mists, corrupted dungeons.

Small group, 2-5:

- Fast content: ganking, roads chests, group dungeons, static dungeon dives,
  faction outposts, Hellgates, tracking, small black-zone roams.
- Best learning: one caller, one healer if possible, one catch/frontline, and
  clear reset rules.

Medium group, 6-12:

- Guild content: roads farming, faction warfare, outposts, black-zone roaming,
  mage raiding, castles/outposts harassment, small ZvZ practice.
- This is where LFG signups and event voice matter. The bot can track attendance
  and later compare fame, deaths, kills, loot, and regear needs.

Large group, 15+:

- CTA, ZvZ, castles, outposts, hideout defense/placement, Avalonian dungeons,
  large faction groups.
- Needs assigned roles, callers, comp checks, loot/regear policy, and discipline.

## Gear And IP Guidance

- IP helps, but it does not replace correct role, correct build, food, potion,
  mount, and defensive timing.
- Weapon IP/spec matters most for damage or healing output from that weapon.
- Armor IP helps survivability and defensive strength.
- Expensive gear without spec and role understanding is just expensive loot for
  enemies.
- For lethal content, bring sets you can replace. For guild content, check the
  event post's minimum IP and comp requirements.
- Food, potion, cape, and mount are part of the build. Missing consumables often
  means the player is not actually ready.

## Zone And Death Rules

- Blue zones are safe from hostile full-loot PvP.
- Yellow zones are mainly knockdown practice and low-risk faction/content.
- Red zones are lethal when hostile flagged players kill you. Watch hostile
  counts and assume transport routes can be camped.
- Black zones are full-loot PvP. There is no hostile count safety net.
- Roads of Avalon are portal-based and volatile. Routes, charges, and TTL matter.
- Mists, Hellgates, Corrupted Dungeons, and other instances can have lethal and
  non-lethal versions. Tell members to check the specific entry/difficulty.

## Making Money

- Silver comes from repeatable systems, not one magic trick.
- Good beginner money: gathering, faction rewards, market buy/sell orders,
  transporting carefully, refining/crafting with focus, dungeons, and loot splits.
- Market/arbitrage posts are targets for buy orders and sell orders. They are
  not a promise that the instant buy/sell price will still exist when someone
  arrives.
- Before transporting, ask: route type, gank risk, mount choice, cargo value,
  escape tools, scout info, and whether the profit is worth the risk.
- Bank often. Profit is not real until it reaches safety.

## PvP Fundamentals

- A kill window is catch, force defensives, purge/wait, pierce/debuff, buff,
  burst, execute, reset.
- Hold damage until the call. Random early damage can trigger defensives and
  weaken the real burst.
- Tanks create the engage or peel. DPS should not run ahead of the catch.
- Healers need the group to stay in range and stop diving out of support.
- Supports win fights by purging, cleansing, buffing, debuffing, protecting the
  caller, and making the enemy's engage fail.
- If the group dies, review positioning, clumping, defensive timing, healer
  range, overchase, cooldowns, and whether the fight was worth taking.

## Ganking And Anti-Ganking

Ganking basics:

- Bring catch, damage, purge, dismount pressure, and mobility.
- Call one target. Do not split damage unless the caller says so.
- Cut escape paths instead of chasing in a straight line forever.
- Do not overchase into guards, hideouts, chokes, or obvious bait.

Anti-ganking basics:

- Stay mounted when possible.
- Watch nametags, roads, chokes, exits, and dismount timers.
- Do not panic-use every cooldown at once.
- Break line of sight, change direction, use terrain, and keep mount/boots ready.
- Transport with a plan, not hope.

## Faction Warfare

- Faction warfare can be safe-ish practice in yellow zones or lethal in red
  zones depending on where the group goes.
- For Martlock groups, players should follow the faction event/LFG channel,
  bring the posted build/IP, join voice, and listen to the caller.
- Bandit-style content and outpost captures reward organization. Random solo
  trickling usually feeds.
- Faction points are not the same as guild season points, but faction groups can
  train comms, movement, and open-world discipline.

## Season Point Mindset

- Season points come from objective-oriented activity, not just standing around.
- Useful guild point pressure includes open-world objectives, castles/outposts,
  crystal/spider style objectives when available, territory/hideout operations,
  mage raiding, and organized black-zone play.
- Members can help at every level by signing up, joining event voice, bringing
  correct gear, scouting, gathering intel, transporting supplies, and showing up
  consistently.
- LFG signups and event voice attendance matter because the bot can turn them
  into real analytics: who came, who stayed, what content drew interest, what
  fame/kills/deaths moved, and what regear may be owed.

## Regear And Event Reports

- If a member wants regear, the cleanest proof is: signed up, joined the event
  voice, died during the event window, and the death matches the content.
- Event reports are best-effort analytics. They use event voice snapshots,
  stored Albion profiles, killboard data, loot input, and officer-entered info.
- The bot cannot perfectly know every detail if players skip signups or never
  join voice.
- Tell members: sign up, join voice, follow the caller, and keep event-related
  deaths/loot tied to the event.

## Answer Examples

Question: "What build should I bring?"

Answer: "Check the event post first. If it has a comp/IP requirement, follow
that. If it says open comp, bring a cheap role you can play: healer, catch tank,
support, or DPS with food/pot/mount. Do not bring expensive gear you cannot
replace."

Question: "How do I stop dying in black zone?"

Answer: "Go cheaper, stay mounted, avoid obvious roads/chokes, bank often, and
stop farming after you see traffic. Bring escape tools and plan exits before you
start. If you die, review what dismounted you and what cooldown you used too
early."

Question: "Can we make money doing this?"

Answer: "Maybe, but only if the risk makes sense. Count expected loot/silver,
gear loss risk, travel time, and whether we can defend the group. For market
routes, use buy orders and sell orders like trading targets, not instant-price
promises."
