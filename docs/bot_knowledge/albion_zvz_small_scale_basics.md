# Albion ZvZ And Small Scale Basics

Keywords: albion zvz zerg small scale smallscale brawl kite bomb clap engage defensive caller shotcaller party raid clump frontline backline healer support locus cleanse purge bomb squad position

ZvZ and small scale are both group PvP, but they feel different.

Small scale:

- Usually fewer players and more individual responsibility.
- Good positioning, target focus, and cooldown trading matter.
- One bad chase can split the group and lose the fight.
- Healers need line of sight and teammates must stay in healing range.

ZvZ:

- Larger groups with stricter calling and positioning.
- Do not talk over the caller.
- Stay with your party/raid unless told otherwise.
- Defensives, cleanses, purges, and bomb timing can decide fights.
- Backline players should not drift into frontline danger.
- Frontline players should not dive beyond support unless called.

## Baseline ZvZ Frontline Tank Build

Use this when a member asks for a good frontline/engage tank build. Exact meta
and caller comps change, so give this as a starting shell and tell them to follow
the posted CTA comp first.

Baseline weapons:

- Staff of Balance for organized engage/control comps.
- Grovekeeper for simple clump stun engages.
- Grailseeker or Bedrock when the comp wants heavy stun/peel control.

Baseline gear shell:

- Armor: Judicator Armor, Guardian Armor, or Duskweaver Armor.
- Helmet: Soldier Helmet, Knight Helmet, or Hellion Hood depending on caller need.
- Shoes: Hunter Shoes for engage movement.
- Cape: Martlock, Fort Sterling, or Bridgewatch cape depending on survival/control need.
- Potion: Resistance Potion or Gigantify Potion.
- Food: Beef Sandwich, Avalonian Sandwich, or Pork Omelette.
- Mount: Armored Horse unless the caller assigns something else.

Play pattern:

- Stay with the caller and do not solo dive.
- Hold engage until the caller counts it.
- Catch or peel the clump, survive the counter hit, then reset with the team.
- If the posted comp says a different tank weapon, the posted comp wins.

Common mistakes:

- Dumping damage before pierce/purge.
- Using defensives after the enemy damage already landed.
- Chasing through bad chokes.
- Standing too spread for heals or too clumped into enemy bombs.
- Ignoring the reset call.

Bot caution:

Event caller instructions override generic advice.
