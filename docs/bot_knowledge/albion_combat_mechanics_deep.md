# Albion Combat Mechanics Deep Reference

Keywords: albion combat mechanics focus fire resilience resilience penetration aoe escalation disarray cluster queue crowd control cc diminishing returns cleanse purge pierce armor magic resist true damage buff cap healing sickness shotcalling kill window

Use this file when members ask why damage feels low, how to melt players, how
large fights work, why clumps explode, why CC fails, or why a comp needs purge,
pierce, cleanse, defensives, and timing.

Core combat idea:

- Albion fights are not just DPS races. Good groups create windows.
- A clean kill window is catch, force defensives, purge or wait, pierce/debuff,
  damage buff, burst, execute, then reset.
- Random early damage can be bad. It can trigger defensives, waste cooldowns,
  and make the real damage window weaker.

Defense and damage:

- Armor and magic resistance reduce incoming physical and magical damage.
- Armor choice changes role profile. Cloth leans damage and healing output,
  leather sits middle, and plate leans survivability/control.
- True damage bypasses normal armor and magic resistance, but some broad damage
  reduction effects may still matter depending on the ability.
- Weapon IP matters most for damage skills. Armor IP matters for survivability,
  defensive scaling, and role durability.

Focus Fire / Resilience:

- When multiple enemy players hit the same target, Albion applies Focus Fire,
  also known as Resilience, to reduce dogpile damage.
- Resilience Penetration lets some weapons ignore part of that protection.
- This is one reason melee brawl and pierce weapons matter in coordinated
  kills. A group should not expect ten random players tapping one target to
  delete it efficiently without a proper window.

AoE Escalation:

- AoE damage can scale upward when hitting multiple enemy players.
- This rewards clean clumps, bombs, choke control, and coordinated AoE dumping.
- The same general idea exists for some AoE crowd-control situations, so clump
  quality and timing matter.

Crowd Control:

- CC includes effects like stun, root, silence, slow, knockback, fear, and other
  control effects depending on ability wording.
- Repeated CC on the same target can become weaker through resistance or
  diminishing behavior.
- Good calls avoid burning every stun early. Catch clean, hold damage, pierce or
  purge, then dump.
- Cleanse tools remove or prevent certain control effects. Save cleanse for
  real enemy engage pressure or for the caller's planned movement.

Buff caps and stat caution:

- Albion has caps on many stats and temporary active bonuses.
- Stacking every damage buff on one player can waste value if caps are reached.
- When exact caps matter, tell members to check the current in-game stat page,
  current wiki page, or caller notes because patch values can change.

Healing and sustain:

- Healers are not only green bars. They manage position, save big cooldowns,
  avoid getting interrupted, and keep the group stable until the next rotation.
- Groups should not outrange heals or dive out of cleanse/defensive coverage.
- Self-healing and repeated healing can have special rules in some contexts, so
  exact values should be verified in game.

Large fight mechanics:

- Disarray affects large-scale Outlands fights and changes how oversized groups
  perform.
- Smart Cluster Queue controls overcrowded zone entry and considers factors such
  as group affiliation, access, premium, and IP.
- Bigger groups need assignments: tanks, defensive tanks, supports, healers,
  ranged damage, melee pressure, battlemounts if used, looters, scouts, and
  callers.

Shotcaller language:

- "Hold damage" means do not reveal the real burst too early.
- "Catching in 3, 2, 1" prepares the group to move together.
- "Pierce now" means lower enemy defenses before damage lands.
- "Purge now" means remove the target's important buff or defensive.
- "Dump" means use the called burst, not every random cooldown.
- "Reset" means stop chasing, stabilize, recover cooldowns, and regroup.

UnionBot answer style:

- For "how do I melt people?", explain the window, not just "get more IP."
- For "why did we not kill them?", ask if damage was held, if purge/pierce
  happened, if defensives were forced, and if people were stacked into Focus
  Fire protection.
- For "why did we get deleted?", ask about clumping, AoE escalation, defensives,
  healer range, cleanse timing, and caller discipline.
