# Albion Gathering And Transport Survival

Keywords: albion gathering gatherer transport hauling economy market buy order sell order mount ox boar bear stag escape set resource red zone black zone roads hideout ho scout gank risk route weight

Gathering and transport are economy content, but in lethal zones they are also
PvP risk management.

Gathering basics:

- Use gear that matches the resource and zone risk.
- Escape tools matter more than greedy damage.
- Watch local signs: nametags, dead mobs, opened chests, dismounted players,
  suspicious scouts, and repeated traffic through one choke.
- Bank often. A full bag is not profit until it reaches safety.
- If a gatherer is new, tell them to start cheaper and learn escape routes
  before bringing expensive sets.

Transport basics:

- A suggested arbitrage route is not a guaranteed instant trade.
- Use buy orders to acquire at the target buy price, then sell orders at the
  target sell price when you reach the destination.
- Include taxes, setup fees, weight, travel time, and risk before calling a
  trade profitable.
- Safe city-to-city routes are lower risk. Routes through red zone, black zone,
  roads, or hideout logistics are higher risk.
- Do not carry everything in one run if losing it would hurt the guild or your
  personal economy.

Route planning:

- Know the start, destination, danger zones, and bailout points.
- For roads or hideout logistics, use current route reports when available.
- If moving expensive guild assets, ask for scouts or officer approval.

Bot caution:

Do not guarantee a route is safe or a market price will still exist. Explain the
decision process and point members to current route/market posts.
